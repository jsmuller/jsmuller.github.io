College of Health Sciences Advising Needs Assessment

The College of Health Sciences at the University of Delaware has a number of fast-growing departments, with two listed in the top 5 largest on campus, Nursing and Kinesiology and Applied Physiology. The majors prepare students for careers in the health care field, or further graduate study in the health professions, which makes these majors extremely popular due to the high number of jobs and the starting salary for health care practioners.

The College utilizes professional advisors to focus on academic planning and preparation for admission to graduate programs, especially in the first couple years of their program, but also to ensure that each student receives the correct and most updated information on policy and procedure when it comes to degree completion. The University is moving in the direction of having professional advisors in every department, however, the College of Health Sciences has already moved to this model for all departments.

For my needs assessment, I plan to focus on what the College of Health Sciences is doing, through surveys from advisors, faculty, and students, and determine what would need to change in order for the advising process to improve and service more students. I also intend to back my suggestions with research on best practices and academic advising theory. I would hope this assessment could also be applied to other departments and colleges at the University who wish to move towards a professional advising model.
